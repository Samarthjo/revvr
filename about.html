about revvr

honest bike guides for india. nothing more, nothing else.

revvr started because every Indian bike review online either reads like a manufacturer press release or buries the real verdict under 3,000 words of spec sheet narration.

we got tired of it. so we built something different.

who we are

we're riders from Bangalore. we spend weekends on bikes we've actually paid for or borrowed from friends — not press loaners with a PR handler standing next to us. we've bought bad bikes. we've made expensive mistakes. revvr is what we wish had existed before we made them.

what we actually do

  • we review bikes the way you'd describe them to a friend over chai — what's good, what's bad, and would we actually buy one
  • we build real price guides by state, because showroom prices are theater
  • we compare bikes for who actually buys them, not for spec sheet fans
  • we curate the best guides from ZigWheels, BikeDekho, BikeWale, and Team-BHP so you don't have to go digging

what we don't do

  • we don't take sponsor money
  • we don't have affiliate links
  • we don't rate mediocre bikes 8/10 because we're scared of the PR team
  • we don't write 2,000 words when 200 will do

the philosophy

if a bike is mediocre, we say it's mediocre. if a price is too high for what you get, we say that too. the goal is to help you make a better decision than we made when we bought our first bikes without doing proper research.
